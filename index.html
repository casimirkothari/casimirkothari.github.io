Casimir Kothari

Email: ckothari (at) bu (dot) edu.

I am a Postdoctoral Associate at Boston University, interested in algebraic geometry and number theory.

I received my PhD at the University of Chicago, where my advisor was Matthew Emerton. My graduate studies were supported by an NSF Graduate Research Fellowship. Previously, I was an undergraduate at Princeton University.

Research

Recently I have been interested in moduli of finite locally free group schemes, and in de Rham cohomology and Hodge theory in positive and mixed characteristic.

  • Arbitrarily large jumps in the de Rham and Hodge cohomology of families in characteristic p . To appear in Journal de Théorie des Nombres de Bordeaux. [pdf] [arXiv]
